Finding your School-Life Balance

As an undergraduate student, taking care of yourself while taking care of your studies can be a daunting task. Fortunately for you, we here at Serenebrum industries are ready to provide you with some of skills and tools you'll need to embark on your post-secondary adventure. Here are some of our best strategies you can use to find that perfect mixture of prioritization, organization, self-care, and self-awareness you'll need for an enjoyable, rewarding post-secondary experience.

Study Tips

Keep a calendar

Create your own calendar, physical or digital, where you can enter all of your schduled class times, due dates for asignments and exams, and schedule in blocks of study time. Set yourself up for success! By scheduling in every course milestone, you already have an idea of direction the course material is headed, and you can stay on track throughout the semester. By scheduling in study time, you can develop a routine so that studying happens naturally and you can hold yourself more accountable in getting your work done.

Don't Forget to Take Breaks (but Don't get Carried Away)

Taking 5 - 10 minute breaks during a study session is a great way to stay on task for longer periods of time without becoming mentally exhausted. Taking a short walk, stretching, even a short meditation can actually help you to be more focused when you return, just make sure that break time isn't suddenly stretching over 2 hours.

Stay Hydrated

Water is life. Make sure you're drinking lots of water throughout to maintain good health and stay clear headed.

Study with Friends

Join or even start a study group with classmates. Collaborating with other people is a great way to see things from new perspectives. Study groups can help keep each other accountable during group study sessions and remind each other of upcoming due dates to stay on track.

Write Notes

Keeping digital notes is great if you have a laptop or some other device you can take to class, but writing out a second set of handwritten notes is an excellent way to reprocess information that may have been delivered quickly during a lecture and reorganizating the information in a way that makes better sense for you. Feel free to get creative! Add colour using highlighters, make a deck of cue cards, create diagrams; find the best way for you to digest the information.

Take Care of You

Don't Ignore Your Personal Needs

It's easy to lose yourself in the study grind, but don't forget to put aside time for your personal life. Burnout is very real, and while it may seem counter-productive to take time for yourself, you'll come back refreshed and renergized!

Get Organized

If you followed our studying advice then you've already got a calendar filled in with your study schedule. Now you know exactly when you can set aside time for yourself, your family, and your friends. Planning ahead is going to be one of the best ways to make sure that you have time set aside from your schoolwork for whatever you need.

Keep Active

Physical activity has many benefits for your mental health as well as your physical health. Make sure to set aside some time every week for your favorite physical activity (:eyes:).

Get Outside

Whether during your free time or if you want to study outdoors, fresh air will help to reinvigorate your soul.

Check out Meditation

Meditation is a great way to ease your mind and find some personal escape.

Check-in with Yourself

School can get overwhelming even without the added stressors of your personal life. Make sure you are consistenly checking in with yourself and addressing any excessive anxiety or negative thoughts. Identifying a problem is always the first step in resolving anything.

Don't Forget to Have Fun

Make you are setting aside even a little time to do the things that you love. Keeping up with your hobbies is still important, even when your neck-deep in due dates.

Ask for Help

Nobody's Perfect

University is hard. Life is hard. Everybody needs help sometimes. Don't ever feel like you can't ask for help, whether it's understanding a concept, falling behind, or dealing with personal matters.

Reach Out to Your Profs

Your professors want you to succeed and they are an amazing resource for helping you understand course concepts. Lectures aren't meant for you to grasp every idea immediately. Most professors want you to ask questions and have office hours set aside for anyone seeking extra guidance.

You're Not Alone

If you are struggling mentally, know that you are not alone and there are resources available you to access. PLease see our resources page for a list of mental health and academic resources available for students.
